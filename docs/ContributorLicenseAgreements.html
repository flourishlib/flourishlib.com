Contributor License Agreements

In order for anyone other than myself to contribute ideas, code or documentation to Flourish, I ask that they sign a contributor license agreement (CLA). This document is used to define the terms under which contributions to Flourish are made. They also could potentially be used in the case of a law suit to help in defense.

Individuals contributing any code, documentation or ideas that they own the rights to should sign the Flourish Contributor License Agreement. If you are submitting intellectual property (IP) owned by your employer, they will need to sign the Flourish Corporate Contributor License Agreement. Please be aware that an individual who is covered under a corporate CLA will also need to submit an individual CLA if they submit any IP that is not owned by their employer.
